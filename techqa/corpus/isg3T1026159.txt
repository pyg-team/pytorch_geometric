Title: IBM Chrome reports obsolete connection settings for WEBGUI - United States

Text:
Chrome; WEBGUI; obsolete TECHNOTE (FAQ)

QUESTION
 Checking WEBGUI connection security in Chrome, we will see the following warning by default:
"The connection to this site uses TLS 1.2 (a strong protocol), RSA (an obsolete key exchange), and AES_128_CBC with HMAC-SHA1 (an obsolete cipher)" 

ANSWER
Customer needs to find common strong cipher supported both in our IBM JRE and their browser.
For our IBM JRE cipher list, please refer to the link below:
https://www.ibm.com/support/knowledgecenter/es/SSYKE2_8.0.0/com.ibm.java.security.component.80.doc/security-component/jsse2Docs/ciphersuites.html?view=embed [https://www.ibm.com/support/knowledgecenter/es/SSYKE2_8.0.0/com.ibm.java.security.component.80.doc/security-component/jsse2Docs/ciphersuites.html?view=embed] 

To check browser supported cipher list, here is the URL on internet:
https://cc.dcsec.uni-hannover.de [https://cc.dcsec.uni-hannover.de/] 

Please compare both list and find common strong cipher, then configure it in server_gui.xml.

For example:
1) stop WEBGUI: egosh service stop WEBGUI.
2) Configure the $EGO_CONFDIR/../../gui/conf/server_gui.xml file as follows:
<ssl id="defaultSSLSettings" keyStoreRef="defaultKeyStore" sslProtocol="${https.protocols}" enabledCiphers="SSL_ECDHE_RSA_WITH_AES_256_GCM_SHA384"/>
3) restart WEBGUI: egosh service start WEBGUI.
