Title: IBM Automatically Starting WebSphere Servers with Autostart Jobs - United States

Text:
WAS; AUTOSTART; WEBSPHERE; QWASSTRSVR; JOB TECHNOTE (TROUBLESHOOTING)

PROBLEM(ABSTRACT)
 To have your IBM WebSphere servers start automatically after you start the subsystem, add autostart job entries for each server you want started. 

RESOLVING THE PROBLEM
To have your IBM WebSphere servers start automatically after you start the subsystem, add autostart job entries for each server you want started. You should do the following: 

 

 *  - [http://www.ibm.com/i/c.gif] Step 1: Create a job description (using the CRTJOBD CL command). Ensure the user is QEJBSVR, ALWMLTTHD is *YES, and the request data is similar to the following line but with the changes to instance and server names. Instance and server parameters are case sensitive. 
   
   V8.5
   Request data . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . : CALL PGM(QWAS85<x>/QWASSTRSVR) PARM('-profilePath' '<profileRoot>' '-server' '<myserver>')
   
   where: <profileRoot> is the fully-qualified path name for the profile, <myserver> is the name of the application server in the instance that you want to start, and <x> is the WAS installation library (typically A). 
   
   Example with profile and server of my85base: Request data . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . : CALL PGM(QWAS85A/QWASSTRSVR) PARM('-profilePath' '/qibm/userdata/websphere/appserver/v85/base/profiles/my85base' '-server' 'my85base') 
   
   V8.0
   Request data . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . : CALL PGM(QWAS8<x>/QWASSTRSVR) PARM('-profilePath' '<profileRoot>' '-server' '<myserver>')
   
   where: <profileRoot> is the fully-qualified path name for the profile, <myserver> is the name of the application server in the instance that you want to start, and <x> is the WAS installation library (typically A). 
   
   Example with profile and server of my8base: Request data . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . : CALL PGM(QWAS8A/QWASSTRSVR) PARM('-profilePath' '/qibm/userdata/websphere/appserver/v8/base/profiles/my8base' '-server' 'my8base') 
   
   V7.0
   Request data . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . : CALL PGM(QWAS7<x>/QWASSTRSVR) PARM('-profilePath' '<profileRoot>' '-server' '<myserver>')
   
   where: <profileRoot> is the fully-qualified path name for the profile, <myserver> is the name of the application server in the instance that you want to start, and <x> is the WAS installation library (typically A). 
   
   Example with profile and server of my7base: Request data . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . : CALL PGM(QWAS7A/QWASSTRSVR) PARM('-profilePath' '/qibm/userdata/websphere/appserver/v7/base/profiles/my7base' '-server' 'my7base') 
   
   V6.1
   Request data . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . : CALL PGM(QWAS61<x>/QWASSTRSVR) PARM('-profilePath' '<profileRoot>' '-server' '<myserver>')
   
   where: <profileRoot> is the fully-qualified path name for the profile, <myserver> is the name of the application server in the instance that you want to start, and <x> is the WAS installation library. To locate the above parameters, run the following QSH command:
   
   /qibm/was/bin/queryWASinstalls
   
   Example with profile and server of my61base: Request data . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . : CALL PGM(QWAS61A/QWASSTRSVR) PARM('-profilePath' '/qibm/userdata/websphere/appserver/v61/base/profiles/my61base' '-server' 'my61base') 
   V6.0
   Request data . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . : QSYS/CALL PGM(QWAS6/QWASSTRSVR) PARM('-profilePath' '<profileRoot>' '-server' '<myserver>')
   
   where: <profileRoot> is the fully-qualified path name for the profile and <myserver> is the name of the application server in the instance that you want to start.
   
   V5.x
   Request data . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . : QSYS/CALL PGM(<library>/<program>) PARM('-instance' '<path>/<myinstance>' '-server' '<myserver>') 
   
   where: <program> is QEJBSTRSVR for the WebSphere Application Server (Base) and Network Deployment or QASESTRSVR for WebSphere Application Server - Express, <myinstance> is the name of your WebSphere Application Server instance, <myserver> is the name of the application server in the instance that you want to start, <library> is the library for the WebSphere Application Server product you are using (refer to the table below), and <path> is the Integrated File System path for your instance according to the WebSphere Application Server product you are using (refer to the table below).
   
   
   
   WebSphere Application Server Version and Edition Library Path 5.0 (Base) QEJBAS5 /QIBM/UserData/WebAS5/Base/ 5.1 (Base) QEJBAS51 /QIBM/UserData/WebAS51/Base/ 5.0 Express QASE5 /QIBM/UserData/WebASE/ASE5/ 5.1 Express QASE51 /QIBM/UserData/WebASE51/ASE/ 


 *  - [http://www.ibm.com/i/c.gif] Step 2: After you create this job description, create an autostart job entry for the WebSphere subsystem by using the ADDAJE CL command with the following parameters:  
   
   Subsystem Description The WebSphere subsystem for the version you are using. 
   
   V5.0 Express: QASE5/QASE5
   V5.0 (Base): QEJBAS5/QEJBAS5
   V5.0 ND: QEJBAS5/QEJBASND5
   V5.1 Express: QASE51/QASE51
   V5.1 (Base): QEJBAS51/QEJBAS5
   V5.1 ND: QEJBAS51/QEJBASND51
   V6 Express, (Base), ND: QWAS6/QWAS6
   V6.1 Express, (Base), ND: QWAS61/QWAS61
   V7.0 Express, Base, ND: QWAS7/QWAS7
   V8.0 Express, Base, ND: QWAS8/QWAS8
   V8.5/V8.5.5 Express Base, ND: QWAS85/QWAS85 Jobname WebSphere server name Job description Job description created above 



Test by ending your subsystem and starting it again. Your server should start.
