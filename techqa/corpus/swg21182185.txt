PROBLEM(ABSTRACT)
 You want to know the end of service date for your WebSphere MQ, MQSeries or other WebSphere Business Integration products. 

RESOLVING THE PROBLEM
 

 * Go to the IBM Software Support Lifecycle page [http://www.ibm.com/software/support/lifecycle/index_a_z.html] - Click on "I [http://www.ibm.com/software/support/lifecycle/index_i.html]" to review the end of support dates for the IBM MQ or IBM Integration Bus products 
   
   - Click on "M [http://www.ibm.com/software/support/lifecycle/index_m.html]" to review the end of support dates for the MQSeries products 
   
   - Click on "W [http://www.ibm.com/software/support/lifecycle/index_w.html]" to review the end of support dates for the WebSphere MQ products
   
   


Notes: 

 * If there is no withdraw from support date, the product is supported, and the end of support date has not been announced.


Renaming history 

 * IBM MQ was previously known as WebSphere MQ prior to version 8.0. 
 *  WebSphere MQ was previously known as MQSeries prior to version 5.3. 
 * WebSphere MQ Workflow was previously known as MQSeries Workflow prior to version 3.4. 
 * WebSphere MQ Everyplace was previously known as MQSeries Everyplace prior to version 2.0 
 * IBM Integration Bus (IIB) was previously known as WebSphere Message Broker prior to IIB V9.0 
 * WebSphere Message Broker (version 6.0) was previously known as WebSphere Business Integration Message Broker (version 5.0) and WebSphere MQ Integrator Broker (version 2.1) 
 * WebSphere Message Broker with Rules and Formatter Extension (version 6.0) was previously known as WebSphere Business Integration Message Broker with Rules and Formatter Extension (version 5.0) and WebSphere MQ Integrator (version 2.1) 
 *  WebSphere Event Broker (version 6.0) was previously known as WebSphere Business Integration Event Broker (version 5.0) and WebSphere MQ Event Broker (version 2.1).
