Withdrawal Notice: 

Technical Support for WebSphere Appliance Management Center (WAMC) will be withdrawn effective Monday 24 August 2015. If you are interested in contributing or leveraging the functionality from WAMC you should consider the open-source Appliance Management Center (AMC) project, available at
https://github.com/ibm-datapower/appliance-management-center [https://github.com/ibm-datapower/appliance-management-center]

WebSphere Appliance Management Center for WebSphere Appliances provides fast time to value, with firmware deployment, domain and service configuration, and deployment policy management capabilities.

Centrally manage appliance firmware and configurations across groups of WebSphere DataPower appliances, and monitor key metrics and events from groups of WebSphere DataPower appliances: 

• Web-based user interface
• Support for multiple generations of WebSphere DataPower appliances
• Firmware management
• Domain and configuration management, including support for deployment policies across multiple environments
• Appliance monitoring
• Operating systems: Windows, Linux, and AIX

Possible uses
WebSphere Appliance Management Center for WebSphere Appliances provides the capability to centrally manage and monitor groups of WebSphere DataPower appliances. 

This offering is intended for deployments with two, or more, WebSphere DataPower appliances, which can span multiple deployment environments (for example, development, test, and production). 

WebSphere Appliance Management Center for WebSphere Appliances serves as the control point for lifecycle management of firmware and configurations across one, or more, groups of appliances. 

Also included is IBM Tivoli Composite Application Manager Agent for WebSphere DataPower Appliance, which you can use to monitor key metrics from multiple appliances in a central location.
